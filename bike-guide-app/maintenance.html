Maintenance

Pre-Ride Checklist

Check these before every single ride. Takes 2 minutes.

Step-by-Step Guides

Chain Cleaning & Lubrication
1

Backpedal and wipe the chain with a dry rag to remove loose dirt.

2

Apply degreaser (or dish soap + water) with a brush or chain cleaning tool. Scrub the chain thoroughly.

3

Rinse with water (avoid high-pressure water on hubs and bottom bracket) and dry completely with a rag.

4

Apply chain lube one drop per inner roller while backpedaling. Let it soak 5 minutes.

5

Wipe off all excess lube — a dry-looking chain is correct. Excess attracts dirt and accelerates wear.

In the Philippines, use WET lube (Finish Line Wet or Squirt) for our humid, sometimes rainy conditions.
Flat Tire Repair
1

Remove the wheel: open quick release or unscrew thru-axle. Shift to smallest cog before removing rear wheel.

2

Use 2 tire levers to pry one side of the tire off the rim. Work around the wheel, not just one spot.

3

Remove the inner tube. Inflate it slightly and listen/feel for the puncture location.

4

IMPORTANT: Run your fingers inside the tire to find the object that caused the flat. Remove it or you'll flat again immediately.

5

Install a new tube OR patch the existing one (sand the area → apply vulcanizing cement → press patch for 60 sec).

6

Re-seat the tire by hand (avoid tire levers — they can pinch the tube). Inflate to correct pressure. Reinstall wheel.

Brake Adjustment
1

Check pad thickness — you should see at least 1mm of pad material. Replace if worn to the metal backing.

2

For mechanical disc brakes: turn the barrel adjuster on the brake lever counter-clockwise to increase braking power.

3

For disc brakes rubbing on one side: loosen the caliper bolts slightly, squeeze the brake lever firmly, then retighten while holding the lever.

4

For hydraulic brakes with spongy feel: you need a brake bleed. This requires bleed kits and is best done at a local bike shop your first time.

Never ride with brakes that feel weak or spongy. This is a safety issue, not a comfort issue.
Gear Adjustment (Cable Tension)
1

Shift to the middle rear cog (cog 4). Pedal and observe if the chain sits cleanly or skips.

2

If shifting UP (toward harder gears) is slow: turn barrel adjuster COUNTER-clockwise by half turns.

3

If shifting DOWN (toward easier gears) is slow: turn barrel adjuster CLOCKWISE by half turns.

4

Test after each half-turn adjustment. Small adjustments make big differences.

5

If no barrel adjustment fixes it, the cable may need replacing. Cable replacement is a bike shop job for beginners.

Maintenance Schedule

Every Ride
  • Pre-ride checklist
  • Wipe down bike
  • Check tire pressure
Weekly
  • Lube chain
  • Check brake pads
  • Inspect tires for cuts
Monthly
  • Deep clean chain
  • Check cable tension
  • Tighten all bolts
  • Check wheel true
Every 2,000 km
  • Replace chain
  • Check cassette
  • Inspect brake pads
  • Check bottom bracket
