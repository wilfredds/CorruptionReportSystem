Gear Guide
Beginner

Climbing Uphill

Shift to an easier gear BEFORE you reach the hill. Keep cadence high (70–80 RPM), don't mash.

Front Ring 1
(22 teeth) Rear Cog 1–3
(34–24t)

If it's a moderate slope, Front Ring 2 + Rear Cog 2–4 works well. Save Ring 1 + Cog 1 for brutal climbs.

Never use Front Ring 1 + Rear Cog 7 — that's cross-chaining and damages your chain and gears.

Flat Road Cruising

The middle chainring is your best friend for flat roads. Fine-tune with the rear cassette for speed vs. comfort.

Front Ring 2
(32 teeth) Rear Cog 3–5
(24–18t)

Start at Rear Cog 4–5 for a comfortable pace. Shift to Cog 6 when you feel strong. Aim for 80–90 RPM cadence.

Going Downhill

Shift up to harder gears to stay efficient and maintain control. Feather your brakes — don't grip and release.

Front Ring 3
(42 teeth) Rear Cog 5–7
(18–12t)

On steep descents, focus on braking control over pedaling. Let gravity do the work. Stay in the drops or lower your center of gravity.

Avoid Front Ring 3 + Rear Cog 1 — cross-chaining. Move front to Ring 2 if you need Cogs 1–2.

Sprint / Speed Burst

Maximum power output. Shift to your biggest front ring and a mid-small rear cog. Stand on the pedals and drive hard.

Front Ring 3
(42 teeth) Rear Cog 4–6
(21–15t)

Stand up, drop your weight into the pedals, and pedal in powerful strokes. Cadence naturally drops during sprints (60–70 RPM) — that's normal.
