Safety Gear
Safety First

Your safety gear is not optional. A ₱1,500 helmet can save your ₱1,500,000 medical bill — or your life.

Must-Have Gear

Cycling Helmet Must Have
₱500 – ₱5,000+

The single most important piece of safety equipment. A helmet reduces risk of head injury by 85%. Any head-impact crash at any speed can be fatal without a helmet. Philippines law (RA 10916) requires helmets on roads.

Where to buy PH: Decathlon, R.O.X., local bike shops (Bike King, Crank PH), Lazada/Shopee. Brands: Giro, Bell, Abus, Nutcase.

Cycling Sunglasses Must Have
₱300 – ₱3,000+

Protects eyes from UV rays (intense in the Philippines), wind, dust, insects, and road debris. A bug or stone in your eye at 25 km/h is a serious safety hazard.

Where to buy PH: Decathlon (Quechua brand is great value), R.O.X., Shopee. Brands: Oakley, Rudy Project, Decathlon.

Highly Recommended

Cycling Gloves Recommended
₱200 – ₱1,500

Protects palms in a crash (instinct is to catch yourself with hands). Reduces vibration fatigue, improves grip, and prevents blisters on long rides.

Where to buy PH: Decathlon, Lazada, Shopee. Any padded fingerless glove works for beginners.

Reflective Vest / Bright Jersey Recommended
₱150 – ₱800

Visibility to drivers is critical, especially in Philippine traffic. A bright neon or reflective vest makes you visible from 200+ meters. Essential for any riding near vehicles, and mandatory for night riding.

Where to buy PH: Construction supply stores (cheapest), bike shops, Shopee. Yellow, orange, or neon green are most visible.

Bike Lights (Front & Rear) Recommended
₱200 – ₱2,000

A front white light and rear red light are required for riding during dawn, dusk, or night. Many Philippine accidents happen in low-light conditions because riders are invisible to drivers.

Where to buy PH: Shopee has affordable sets starting at ₱200. Cateye, Lezyne, and Bontrager for quality.

Nice to Have

Knee & Elbow Pads Optional
₱300 – ₱2,000

Important for MTB trail riding or if you're learning technical skills. For road/flat riding, they're less critical but add peace of mind for beginners.

Road Rules for Philippine Cyclists

Ride on the rightmost lane or designated bike lane. Never ride against traffic.

Signal turns using hand signals — left arm out = left turn, right arm out = right turn.

Helmets are required by law (RA 10916) on public roads. You can be fined without one.

Do not use earphones while riding — you need to hear traffic, horns, and approaching vehicles.

Give way to pedestrians at crossings. Stop for red lights — yes, even on a bicycle.

Avoid riding 2-abreast on busy roads. Single file when traffic is present.

Be visible — wear bright colors during the day and use lights at night and at dawn/dusk.
