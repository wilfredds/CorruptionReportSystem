Quick Facts

🏔️ Mountain Bike

  • Flat/riser handlebars
  • Wide knobby tires (2.1–2.4")
  • Front suspension fork
  • 3×7 or 1×12 gears
  • Flat pedals or clipless
  • Lower saddle position
  • More upright posture
  • Works on: dirt, gravel, paved

🛣️ Road Bike

  • Drop handlebars
  • Narrow smooth tires (23–28mm)
  • No suspension
  • 2×11 or 1×12 gears
  • Clipless pedals (required)
  • High saddle position
  • Aggressive aero posture
  • Works on: paved roads only

Which one is for you?

Choose MTB if: You want versatility, ride both roads and trails, prioritize comfort and durability, are a beginner, or ride in areas with rough roads (common in the Philippines).

Choose Road Bike if: You ride exclusively on paved roads, want maximum speed and efficiency, plan to do long-distance events like gran fondos or centuries, and are willing to maintain it more carefully.

💡 Philippine Cycling Context

For most Filipino beginners, an MTB is the smarter first bike. Our roads vary wildly — from smooth BGC paths to rough provincial roads. MTBs handle everything. Road bikes are faster on good tarmac but suffer on typical PH road conditions.

Cycling for 30 minutes burns 250–400 calories depending on your weight and intensity.

Regular cycling reduces the risk of depression and anxiety by up to 48% according to research.

Cycling is low-impact — it's 3× gentler on your joints compared to running the same distance.

The Philippines has a fast-growing cycling community. Facebook cycling groups in Metro Manila have over 500,000 combined members.

Replacing one car trip per day with cycling saves approximately 1.3 tons of CO₂ per year.

Professional road cyclists can maintain 40–50 km/h for hours. Beginners average 15–20 km/h.

Cycling primarily works: quadriceps, hamstrings, calves, glutes, and core. It's a near-total body workout.

UV exposure in the Philippines is extreme — SPF 50+ sunscreen is recommended for any ride over 30 minutes.

A decent beginner MTB in the Philippines costs ₱7,000–₱20,000. Road bikes start at ₱20,000+.

The Tour de France is 3,500 km long. The race record average speed is 41 km/h. Riding is not required.
