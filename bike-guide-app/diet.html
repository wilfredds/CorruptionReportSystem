Diet & Nutrition
Timing

Eat a proper meal 2–3 hours before riding, or a light snack 30–60 minutes before.

Sinangag + Itlog (Garlic Rice + Egg)
A classic Filipino pre-ride meal. Complex carbs from rice provide sustained energy. Eggs add protein. Add a banana for a potassium boost.
2–3 hrs before
Banana (Saging)
The cyclist's best friend. Easy to digest, rich in potassium (prevents cramps), and provides quick energy. Available everywhere in the Philippines for ₱5–₱15.
30–60 min before
Pandesal with Peanut Butter
A quick and affordable Filipino snack. Carbs from bread + protein/fat from peanut butter = good sustained energy for shorter rides under 1 hour.
30–60 min before
Coffee (with caution)
Caffeine can improve performance. But coffee also dehydrates — make sure you drink extra water if you have coffee before a ride. Best for morning rides.
45–60 min before
⚠️ Avoid: Heavy oily food (lechon, sisig) within 3 hours of riding. High-fat meals slow digestion and cause side stitches while cycling.
Rule of Thumb

For rides under 60 min, water is enough. For rides over 60 min, add 30–60g of carbs per hour.

Banana
30g of carbs, easy to eat on the bike, no packaging mess. Carry in your jersey pocket. The perfect on-the-bike food for Filipino cyclists.
Every 45 min on long rides
Coconut Water (Buko Juice)
Natural isotonic drink — replaces electrolytes lost in sweat (sodium, potassium, magnesium). Available fresh throughout the Philippines. More natural than sports drinks.
During long rides
Energy Gels / Glucose Tablets
For rides over 90 min, energy gels provide quick carbohydrates. GU Energy, Maurten, or generic glucose tablets. Take with water — never without.
Every 45 min after hour 1
Bread / Sponge Cake (Mamón)
Filipino pro cyclists often use mamón or simple bread as on-bike food. Cheap, available everywhere, and provides carbs. Cut into small pieces before riding.
For 2+ hour rides
Recovery Window

Eat within 30 minutes of finishing your ride — this is the optimal recovery window for muscle repair.

Canned Tuna + Rice
The perfect budget Filipino recovery meal. Protein from tuna rebuilds muscle. Carbs from rice replenish glycogen. Affordable, quick to prepare.
Within 30 min
3 Eggs (any style)
20g of high-quality protein. Contains all essential amino acids. One of the most efficient and affordable recovery foods available. Eggs also contain leucine which triggers muscle protein synthesis.
Post-ride meal
Chocolate Milk
Yes, chocolate milk! Research shows it's one of the best recovery drinks — the ideal carb-to-protein ratio (4:1), affordable, and available at every sari-sari store.
Within 30 min
Chicken + Sweet Potato
For serious riders: lean protein from chicken + complex carbs from kamote (sweet potato) = optimal recovery combination. Great post-training meal for growing cyclists.
Main post-ride meal
⚡ Avoid alcohol post-ride — it blocks glycogen replenishment and slows muscle recovery significantly.

Water — The Foundation
Drink 500ml–750ml in the 2 hours before a ride. During riding: 500–750ml per hour (more in Philippine heat). Don't wait until you're thirsty — by then you're already 2% dehydrated.
Electrolytes
Sweat = lost sodium, potassium, magnesium. For rides over 60 min, add electrolytes: coconut water, sports drinks, or electrolyte tablets. Electrolyte loss (not just water loss) causes cramps.

Philippine Heat Warning

Riding in Philippine summer heat (March–May) can cause you to sweat 1–2 liters per hour. In these conditions, increase fluid intake by 25–50% compared to cooler weather. Ride early morning or late afternoon to reduce heat stress.

Calamansi Water
Squeeze 3–4 calamansi into water with a pinch of salt and a teaspoon of sugar — a natural homemade electrolyte drink. Tastes great, costs almost nothing, and works brilliantly for Filipino cyclists.
